American Time Use Survey Averages for Full Time Students


Key to text colors:
Full time students--men:    Full time students--women: 
Looking at the data this way is interesting for several reasons. First, I was interested in the time each gender spent with grooming. As may be expected, women spend longer than men on average on this activity--about 20 minutes per day. I, however, spent an average of two hours per day on this activity over the course of two weeks. I do not dispute that I probably spend longer doing this than most people, but I also wonder at whether the survvey methodology would skew these results somewhat. Are people actively remembering how much time they spend on personal care? I think maybe not. This was one activity with one of the highest "entry numbers"--meaning that I started and stopped somewhat randomly and intermixed with other activities. For this reason, I think that people may not be remembering every time they brush their teeth, etc.
I also thought it was interesting looking at the educational data. Men spent slightly longer, on average, on homework than women and slightly more time in class. I spent longer per day on average on both of these activities than either of these two groups.
